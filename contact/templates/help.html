Open a support request

The Peterborough Tenants Union is a membership organisation. Just like trade unions, this means that we only provide help to people who are members.

We can offer basic advice to non-members, and of course you can read the advice and guidance pages on this website.

If you need more 'hands-on' support and you are a member (or are interested in becoming a member) you can send a support request, and we'll get back to you as soon as possible.

Other organisations

You can also get help from various other organisations. We've included a list below of the most helpful ones.

Click on the organisation name to visit their website.

In an emergency call 999.
It's an emergency if someone is in danger or a crime is being committed.
You can also use the European number 112.
Shelter
A charity dedicated to preventing homelessness. They provide legal advice and support to people who are being evicted.
Citizens Advice
A legal advice charity, online and in high-street branches.
Samaritans
Call 116 123
Samaritans are there to listen if you are experiencing stress, suicidal thoughts, or just feeling overwhelmed.
Women's Aid
Women's aid support people who may be affected by domestic violence. They provide support, advice and training.
Refuge
A national domestic abuse charity.
Mens Domestic Abuse Advice Line
The Helpline for male victims of domestic abuse.
Switchboard
Switchboard is the national LGBTQIA+ support line.
Stepchange
Debt advice charity.

You may also be able to get support and advice from trade unions, many have free legal helplines for members.

You should also speak to your Local Council housing team as soon as you think you are at risk of becoming homeless (i.e. as soon as you receive an eviction letter, if you have nowhere to move to) so that they can advise you on options.
